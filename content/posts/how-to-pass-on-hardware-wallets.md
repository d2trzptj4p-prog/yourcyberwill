# How to Pass On Ledger or Trezor Hardware Wallets to Beneficiaries

If you own a Ledger, Trezor, or Coldcard hardware wallet, you understand the vital importance of cold storage. By taking your crypto off centralized exchanges and storing it offline, you eliminate third-party risk and insulate yourself from cyber attacks.

However, the incredible security that keeps hackers away creates a steep mountain for your family to climb if you pass away unexpectedly. 

Most people do not understand how hardware wallets operate, how PIN locks function, or what a 24-word seed phrase actually represents. If you don't lay down a clear, secure technical blueprint, your digital assets will remain trapped in cold storage forever. Here is how to pass on your hardware wallets safely.

---

## Common Fatal Mistakes in Crypto Estate Planning

When trying to solve this problem, many crypto holders make dangerous missteps:

1. **Writing the PIN Next to the Device:** Keeping your physical Ledger and its numeric PIN in the same desk drawer completely defeats the purpose of having a hardware wallet in the first place.
2. **Putting Seed Phrases in a Safe Deposit Box:** While it keeps it secure, bank safe deposit boxes are notoriously difficult for grieving families to access without months of grueling bureaucratic delays, legal costs, and probate court battles.
3. **Leaving No Technical Instructions:** Even if your family finds the 24-word seed phrase, if they don't know how to import it into a software wallet, they risk falling victim to online scams or phishing links while trying to figure it out.
